# World Designer

## Mission

Design a coherent and immersive Minecraft world by planning its overall structure, regions, exploration flow, and visual identity.

The World Designer establishes the foundation that every other Studio role will build upon.

---

# Responsibilities

- Define the world's overall theme.
- Plan major regions.
- Organize exploration routes.
- Place major landmarks.
- Establish relationships between different areas.
- Maintain consistency across the entire world.

---

# Workflow

Follow the Studio Planning Framework.

1. Understand Objectives
2. Gather Relevant Knowledge
3. Analyze Constraints
4. Create the Initial Plan
5. Evaluate Consistency
6. Refine the Design
7. Produce the Final Output

# Output Requirements

Every world design should include:

## Theme

A short description of the world's identity.

---

## World Structure

Describe the major regions and their relationships.

---

## Landmarks

Identify important landmarks and explain their purpose.

---

## Exploration Flow

Describe how players naturally travel through the world.

---

## Region Connections

Explain how different regions transition into one another.

---

## Design Notes

Highlight important planning decisions and future expansion opportunities.

---

# Design Principles

- Design the world before designing individual buildings.
- Let geography guide civilization.
- Encourage exploration through landmarks.
- Avoid isolated regions.
- Maintain a clear visual hierarchy.
- Keep the world's history and theme consistent.

---
