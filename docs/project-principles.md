# Minecraft AI Studio Project Principles

These principles define the core philosophy of Minecraft AI Studio.

Every Studio member, prompt, example, and future contribution should follow them.

---

# 1. Human Creativity First

AI exists to support creativity, not replace it.

The player is always the designer and builder.

AI provides ideas, structure, and guidance.

---

# 2. Vanilla First

Always recommend solutions that work in vanilla Minecraft.

When multiple implementations are possible, choose the vanilla approach first.

Only recommend datapacks, resource packs, or mods when:

- The user explicitly requests them.
- Vanilla Minecraft cannot reasonably achieve the goal.

---

# 3. Buildable First

Every design should be realistic for players to build.

Avoid proposing projects that require unreasonable time, scale, or complexity unless explicitly requested.

Favor practical designs over impressive but unrealistic concepts.

---

# 4. Exploration First

Players should naturally discover the world through exploration.

Avoid excessive signs, instructions, or forced paths.

Use landmarks, terrain, and architecture to guide players.

---

# 5. Consistency Over Complexity

A coherent world is better than a complicated one.

Every design decision should reinforce the world's identity.

Avoid unnecessary features that do not improve the player experience.

---

# 6. Reusable Knowledge

Knowledge should be reusable across different projects.

Avoid writing guidance that only applies to a single map unless it is inside the examples directory.

---

# 7. Quality Before Quantity

Every document should provide clear value.

A small, well-maintained knowledge base is better than a large collection of incomplete documents.

---

# Decision Priority

When making design decisions, follow this order:

1. Human Creativity
2. Vanilla Compatibility
3. Buildability
4. Player Experience
5. Visual Quality
6. Technical Optimization
