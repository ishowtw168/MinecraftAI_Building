# Objective

Transform the world design into a complete theme park experience.

Every attraction should support the island's identity while creating a balanced day of exploration, excitement, and relaxation.

---

# Guest Journey

The intended experience follows this rhythm:

Arrival

↓

Orientation

↓

Exploration

↓

Major Attraction

↓

Rest

↓

Exploration

↓

Major Attraction

↓

Departure

Players should always have a clear destination without feeling forced onto a single path.

# Queue Design

Queues should tell part of the story.

Examples

Fantasy Kingdom

Players pass through:

- Castle gardens
- Libraries
- Magical laboratories

Ancient Desert

Players pass through:

- Excavation sites
- Ancient tombs
- Forgotten temples

Waiting should feel like exploration.

---

# Crowd Distribution

Avoid placing major attractions too close together.

Each island should include:

- One major attraction
- One secondary attraction
- One observation point
- One rest area

This encourages players to explore the entire island.

---

# Accessibility

Players who do not enjoy rides should still experience:

- Scenic viewpoints
- Hidden paths
- Interactive environments
- Story elements

---

# Design Principles

- Attractions support the environment.
- Exploration is always rewarded.
- Rest areas are placed naturally.
- Every attraction has a clear visual identity.
- Queue areas are part of the storytelling.

---

# Recommendations for Story Writer

Develop a narrative connecting both islands.

The story should explain why these worlds exist together while preserving each island's unique identity.
