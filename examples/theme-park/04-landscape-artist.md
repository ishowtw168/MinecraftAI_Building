- Project Brief

---

# Objective

Shape the natural environment to support exploration, reinforce each island's identity, and enhance the player experience.

The landscape should guide movement, frame landmarks, and create memorable scenery.

---

# Fantasy Kingdom

## Terrain

- Gentle rolling hills
- Small rivers
- Waterfalls flowing from the floating island
- Cliffs overlooking the void
- Open meadows surrounding the castle

Terrain should feel welcoming and encourage exploration.

---

- Fallen Logs

## Natural Landmarks

---

# Sightlines

Players should always be able to identify a major landmark.

Avoid dense forests or tall terrain that completely block important structures.

Key views:

- Castle visible from the Central Hub
- Pyramid visible across the park
- Giant Tree visible from Fantasy Kingdom
- Oasis visible from the desert entrance

---

# Environmental Storytelling

Fantasy Kingdom should feel alive.

Examples:

- Animals grazing in fields.
- Flowers surrounding old ruins.
- Small campsites near paths.

Ancient Desert should tell the story of a forgotten civilization.

Examples:

- Collapsed statues.
- Buried walls.
- Abandoned excavation sites.

---

# Design Principles

- Nature supports architecture.
- Every path reveals a new view.
- Major landmarks remain visible.
- Terrain changes gradually.
- Exploration is rewarded.

---

# Recommendations for Theme Park Designer

Focus on placing attractions where they naturally fit within the landscape.

Rides should enhance the environment rather than dominate it.
