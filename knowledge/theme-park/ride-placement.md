# Ride Placement

Ride placement determines where attractions are located within a theme park. Proper placement creates balanced exploration, improves navigation, and maintains a consistent guest experience.

---

# Objectives

- Distribute guests across the park.
- Create clear visual landmarks.
- Balance excitement and relaxation.
- Encourage exploration of every area.

---

# Attraction Hierarchy

## Major Attractions

Large signature rides that define a themed area.

Examples

- Roller Coaster
- Castle
- Giant Ferris Wheel

---

## Supporting Attractions

Medium-sized rides that complement major attractions.

Examples

- Pirate Ship
- Tea Cups
- Dark Ride

---

## Minor Attractions

Small experiences that fill empty spaces.

Examples

- Food Stalls
- Mini Games
- Photo Spots
- Street Performances

---

# Design Principles

- Give each themed area one major attraction.
- Keep similar rides grouped together.
- Avoid placing major attractions too close to each other.
- Leave open space around large rides.
- Use smaller attractions to connect larger ones.

---

# Spacing Guidelines

Major Attraction

- Large visual impact
- Visible from long distances

Supporting Attraction

- Within walking distance of a major attraction

Minor Attraction

- Fill gaps between destinations

---

# Common Mistakes

- Placing all major rides near the entrance.
- Leaving large empty spaces.
- Mixing unrelated themes.
- Creating long walks without attractions.
