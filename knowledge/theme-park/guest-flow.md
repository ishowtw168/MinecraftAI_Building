# Guest Flow

Guest flow is the movement of players through a theme park. A well-designed flow guides exploration naturally, reduces confusion, and ensures that every major attraction receives attention.

---

# Objectives

- Guide players naturally.
- Reduce unnecessary backtracking.
- Prevent crowded areas.
- Highlight major landmarks.
- Encourage exploration.

---

# Flow Types

## Linear

Players follow a fixed route.

Best for:

- Story-driven experiences
- Haunted houses
- Escape rooms

---

## Loop

Players return to the starting area without retracing their steps.

Best for:

- Theme parks
- Zoos
- Museums

---

## Hub and Spoke

A central hub connects multiple themed areas.

Best for:

- Large parks
- Adventure worlds
- RPG maps

---

# Design Principles

- Make the main path easy to recognize.
- Place landmarks where they are visible from a distance.
- Avoid long empty corridors.
- Give players meaningful choices at intersections.
- Ensure every path leads to something interesting.

---

# Common Flow Problems

- Dead ends with no reward.
- Overlapping routes that confuse players.
- Attractions hidden from the main path.
- Long stretches without points of interest.

---

# Suitable Projects

- Theme Parks
- Adventure Maps
- RPG Worlds
- Exhibition Areas
