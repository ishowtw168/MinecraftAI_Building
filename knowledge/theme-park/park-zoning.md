# Park Zoning

Park zoning divides a theme park into distinct themed areas. Good zoning improves navigation, strengthens immersion, and helps players understand the park's overall structure.

---

# Objectives

- Organize attractions by theme.
- Create clear transitions between areas.
- Improve navigation.
- Maintain a consistent atmosphere.

---

# Zone Types

## Entry Zone

The first impression of the park.

Typical Features

- Main Gate
- Information Center
- Shops
- Restaurants

---

## Adventure Zone

Focused on exploration and excitement.

Typical Attractions

- Roller Coasters
- Adventure Trails
- Interactive Experiences

---

## Family Zone

Designed for relaxed gameplay.

Typical Attractions

- Carousel
- Mini Games
- Food Court

---

## Landmark Zone

The visual centerpiece of the park.

Typical Attractions

- Castle
- Giant Tree
- Pyramid
- Ferris Wheel

---

# Design Principles

- Give each zone a unique identity.
- Use natural or architectural transitions between zones.
- Keep attractions consistent with the zone theme.
- Ensure each zone has at least one major landmark.
- Balance ride density across the park.

---

# Transition Methods

- Bridges
- Tunnels
- Forests
- Rivers
- Elevation Changes
- Gateways

---

# Common Mistakes

- Mixing unrelated themes.
- Abrupt transitions.
- Uneven attraction distribution.
- Zones without clear identities.

---

# Suitable Projects

- Theme Parks
- Resort Worlds
- Adventure Maps
- Exhibition Worlds
