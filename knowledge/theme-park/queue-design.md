# Queue Design

Queue design manages how players wait before experiencing an attraction. A well-designed queue maintains immersion, prevents boredom, and builds anticipation for the main experience.

---

# Objectives

- Maintain player engagement.
- Build anticipation.
- Support smooth guest flow.
- Hide waiting time through environmental storytelling.

---

# Queue Types

## Open Queue

Players wait in an open area.

Best for

- Small attractions
- Low-capacity rides

---

## Switchback Queue

A back-and-forth path that efficiently uses space.

Best for

- Popular attractions
- Indoor rides

---

## Themed Queue

The queue itself is part of the attraction.

Best for

- Dark rides
- Story-driven experiences
- Escape rooms

---

# Design Principles

- Keep the queue visually interesting.
- Reveal the attraction gradually.
- Add scenery, props, or interactive elements.
- Avoid long straight corridors.
- Ensure the entrance and exit are clearly separated.

---

# Queue Elements

- Signage
- Lighting
- Decorative props
- Interactive displays
- Storytelling scenes
- Sound effects

---

# Common Mistakes

- Empty waiting areas.
- Queue paths that are difficult to follow.
- Immediate visibility of the entire attraction.
- Entrance and exit crossing each other.

---

# Suitable Projects

- Theme Parks
- Adventure Maps
- Escape Rooms
- Story-based Attractions
