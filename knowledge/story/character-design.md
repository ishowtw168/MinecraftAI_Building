# Character Design

Character design creates memorable individuals who make the world feel alive. Whether they are heroes, villains, merchants, or villagers, every character should have a clear purpose within the world.

---

# Objectives

- Give the world personality.
- Support storytelling.
- Guide player interaction.
- Strengthen the identity of each location.

---

# Character Roles

## Hero

Drives the main story or assists the player.

Examples

- Knight
- Explorer
- Adventurer

---

## Villain

Creates conflict and challenges.

Examples

- Dark Lord
- Corrupted King
- Pirate Captain

---

## Merchant

Provides resources and information.

Examples

- Blacksmith
- Shopkeeper
- Traveling Trader

---

## Resident

Makes the world feel inhabited.

Examples

- Farmer
- Fisher
- Miner
- Scholar

---

# Design Principles

- Give every character a clear role.
- Match the character to the world's theme.
- Design locations that reflect the character's lifestyle.
- Make important characters visually distinct.
- Let characters contribute to the world's story.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Characters without purpose.
- Generic NPCs that all feel the same.
- Characters that conflict with the world's theme.
- Important characters that are difficult to recognize.

---

# Suitable Projects

- RPG Worlds
- Adventure Maps
- Theme Parks
- Story-driven Experiences
- Survival Worlds
