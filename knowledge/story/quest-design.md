# Quest Design

Quest design gives players clear objectives while encouraging exploration and interaction with the world. Good quests feel like a natural part of the environment rather than a list of tasks.

---

# Objectives

- Encourage exploration.
- Give players meaningful goals.
- Support the world's story.
- Reward curiosity and discovery.

---

# Quest Types

## Exploration

Discover new locations.

Examples

- Find a hidden temple.
- Reach the highest mountain.
- Explore an abandoned village.

---

## Collection

Gather specific items.

Examples

- Ancient relics
- Lost books
- Rare materials

---

## Puzzle

Solve environmental challenges.

Examples

- Open a hidden passage.
- Activate an ancient mechanism.
- Complete a park attraction puzzle.

---

## Combat

Defeat enemies or survive encounters.

Examples

- Defeat a dungeon boss.
- Protect a village.
- Clear a monster camp.

---

# Design Principles

- Give every quest a clear purpose.
- Lead players toward interesting locations.
- Match rewards with the effort required.
- Connect quests to the world's history.
- Avoid repetitive objectives.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Repetitive fetch quests.
- Objectives with unclear directions.
- Rewards that feel meaningless.
- Quests unrelated to the world.

---

# Suitable Projects

- RPG Worlds
- Adventure Maps
- Theme Parks
- Escape Rooms
- Story-driven Experiences
