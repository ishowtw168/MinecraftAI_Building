# Worldbuilding

Worldbuilding is the process of creating a believable and consistent world. A well-designed world gives purpose to locations, strengthens immersion, and encourages players to explore beyond individual builds.

---

# Objectives

- Create a coherent world.
- Give locations meaning.
- Support exploration through context.
- Connect different regions with a shared identity.

---

# Core Elements

## Theme

The central concept that defines the world.

Examples

- Nordic Mythology
- Ancient Egypt
- Steampunk
- Floating Islands

---

## History

Describe how the world became what it is today.

Questions

- Who built this place?
- What happened here?
- Why does it exist?

---

## Geography

Explain how the environment influences the world.

Examples

- Kingdoms built around rivers.
- Cities protected by mountains.
- Islands connected by bridges.

---

## Culture

Define how people live in the world.

Examples

- Architecture
- Clothing
- Religion
- Technology
- Traditions

---

# Design Principles

- Keep the world internally consistent.
- Let geography influence civilization.
- Give every major location a purpose.
- Connect stories across multiple regions.
- Support visual storytelling through the environment.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Mixing unrelated themes.
- Creating locations without purpose.
- Ignoring the world's history.
- Making every area feel the same.

---
