# Environmental Storytelling

Environmental storytelling is the practice of telling a story through the environment instead of direct narration. Players learn about the world by observing architecture, objects, landscapes, and the condition of each location.

---

# Objectives

- Tell stories without dialogue.
- Reward careful observation.
- Strengthen immersion.
- Make every location feel meaningful.

---

# Storytelling Elements

## Architecture

Buildings reflect the people who built them.

Examples

- A ruined castle suggests a fallen kingdom.
- A fortified village indicates frequent attacks.

---

## Objects

Small details reveal history.

Examples

- Broken weapons
- Abandoned camps
- Scattered books
- Burned wagons

---

## Environment

Nature changes over time.

Examples

- Overgrown ruins
- Flooded tunnels
- Collapsed bridges
- Dead forests

---

## Lighting

Lighting influences emotion.

Examples

- Warm lighting creates safety.
- Dim lighting creates tension.
- Colored lighting suggests magical energy.

---

# Design Principles

- Show instead of explain.
- Keep visual details consistent with the world's history.
- Reward players who explore carefully.
- Let every location tell a small story.
- Use environmental clues to connect different areas.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Explaining everything with signs.
- Adding random decorations without purpose.
- Ignoring the world's established history.
- Repeating the same storytelling techniques everywhere.

---

# Suitable Projects

- RPG Worlds
- Adventure Maps
- Theme Parks
- Escape Rooms
- Story-driven Experiences
