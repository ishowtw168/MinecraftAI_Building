# Visual Hierarchy

## Overview

Visual hierarchy is the arrangement of elements so players naturally notice the most important features first.

Good visual hierarchy guides attention without using signs or instructions.

---

# Why It Matters

Players should instinctively know:

- Where to go.
- What is important.
- What is worth exploring.

A good world communicates through its design.

---

# Levels of Attention

## Primary Focus

The first thing players notice.

Examples:

- Castle
- Giant tree
- Roller coaster
- Mountain
- Lighthouse

---

## Secondary Focus

Important supporting elements.

Examples:

- Village
- Bridges
- Rivers
- Towers

---

## Background

Everything that supports the scene without competing for attention.

Examples:

- Forest
- Hills
- Small houses
- Decorations

---

# Techniques

## Height

Taller structures naturally attract attention.

---

## Contrast

Use different colors, materials, or lighting.

---

## Shape

Unique silhouettes are memorable.

---

## Lighting

Bright areas attract players, especially at night.

---

## Framing

Use roads, trees, cliffs, or buildings to frame important landmarks.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Every building has the same height.
- Too many landmarks compete for attention.
- No clear focal point.
- Excessive visual noise.

---

# AI Design Checklist

□ Can players identify the most important location within five seconds?

□ Is there only one primary focal point?

□ Do secondary elements support rather than compete?

□ Does lighting reinforce the hierarchy?
