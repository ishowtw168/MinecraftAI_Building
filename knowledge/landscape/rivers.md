# Rivers

Rivers connect different regions of a Minecraft world, guide player exploration, and create natural transitions between biomes. They often serve as transportation routes, landmarks, and the foundation for settlements.

---

# Characteristics

- Flow through multiple regions
- Connect lakes and oceans
- Natural travel routes
- Define terrain boundaries
- Support nearby vegetation
- Create scenic landscapes

---

# Terrain Features

## River Source

- Mountain springs
- Snowmelt
- Small lakes

## River Channel

- Gentle curves
- Varying widths
- Sand and gravel banks

## River Mouth

- Delta
- Estuary
- Ocean connection

---

# Common Blocks

## Water

- Water
- Seagrass
- Kelp

## River Bank

- Sand
- Gravel
- Dirt
- Grass Block

## Decoration

- Sugar Cane
- Lily Pads
- Moss
- Small Rocks

---

# Design Principles

- Avoid perfectly straight rivers.
- Follow the natural shape of the terrain.
- Vary the width along the river.
- Add bridges only at logical crossing points.
- Place settlements near calm sections of the river.
